Our Retaining Wall Installation Process

Consultation and Site Assessment

We start by visiting your property to evaluate the landscape, including soil conditions, slope, and drainage. During this step, we also discuss your goals—whether it's erosion control, leveling, or enhancing your outdoor aesthetics.

Custom Design and Material Selection

Our team creates a retaining wall design tailored to your property. Each option is explained to ensure you choose the right fit for your landscape and budget.

Preparation and Excavation

The site is prepared by clearing debris, leveling the area, and excavating to create a stable and durable foundation. We also lay a base of compacted gravel to prevent shifting and enhance structural stability.

Wall Construction and Drainage Installation

Using precision techniques, we construct the retaining wall layer by layer, ensuring each level is perfectly aligned. A critical part of this step is integrating proper drainage systems, such as perforated pipes, gravel backfill, or weep holes, to prevent water buildup and ensure long-term performance.

Final Walkthrough

Once construction is complete, we walk you through the finished project to ensure it meets your expectations. We review key features, provide maintenance tips, and address any final questions, ensuring you’re fully satisfied before we consider the job done.

Retaining Walls: Concrete Block or Poured Concrete

Choosing between concrete block and poured concrete retaining walls depends on your project's needs, budget, and design goals.

Concrete Block Walls

Concrete block walls are cost-effective for smaller projects and offer faster installation with a variety of styles and finishes. They're ideal for curved designs and are easier to repair or modify. However, they have height limitations, may need more maintenance, and are less suitable for heavy loads or areas prone to water damage.

Pros: Affordable, quick installation, versatile designs

Cons: Height limits, more maintenance, water susceptibility

Poured Concrete Walls

Poured concrete walls provide superior strength and durability, making them ideal for large-scale or complex projects. They resist water damage better and require less long-term maintenance. On the downside, they have higher upfront costs, take longer to install, and repairs can be more challenging.

Pros: Durable, water-resistant, low maintenance

Cons: Costly, longer installation, complex repairs

How Much Does a Retaining Wall Cost in Houston, TX?

The cost of a retaining wall in Houston, TX, can vary depending on several factors, including the materials used, the size and height of the wall, and the complexity of the design. On average, homeowners in Houston can expect to pay between $20 and $50 per square foot for a professionally installed retaining wall.
